Optimizing Research on Construction Projects

About The Project

Context: Cordell Connect is Australia's largest database for residential, commercial, and mining projects. It is an essential tool to get data and find leads, helping service providers and the industry.

The Problem: Cordell research is an internal department of specialists researchers that conducted their data acquisition process through a legacy system that made their work cumbersome and slow, affecting the speed of data to market.

The Process & Outcome: I led the team to explore ways to optimize their tool and processes so we could provide up-to-date information to the industry. Through design thinking, we managed to understand causes, frame problems, and explore solutions. Outcome: we managed to serve data 4 times faster.

Solutions & Outcome

Reduced Double Entry & Confusion

Research showed us that the user needed constant communication with their colleagues so they could synchronize their work. Different means of communicating between colleagues led to an increase of project duplicates, double work and confusion. After several idea generation workshops, we chose to build the messaging widget, one of the most used and cherished pieces of the new system.

Improved Work Efficiency By Tracking Jobs

Users were surrounded by piles of papers and notes in order to keep track of their projects. To help them out , we created a calendar section: a project view sorted in a calendar way to remind them whom to call and when.

Better Usability & Data Granularity

As customers wanted more details on upcoming projects, we re-architectured the data adding granularity. In addition, we improved the usability of the forms with new patterns & dynamic fields.
